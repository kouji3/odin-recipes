Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

Description

Spaghetti Aglio e Olio is a traditional Italian pasta dish from Naples. It's arguably one of the simplest and most satisfying pasta recipes out there. Aglio e Olio means 'garlic and oil' in Italian, and indeed, the dish involves little more than these along with pasta and a pinch of chili flakes. Despite its simplicity, it's incredibly delicious and deeply comforting. The pasta is cooked until al dente and then tossed in a flavor-packed mixture of saut√©ed garlic, olive oil, and red chili flakes. It's usually topped off with a sprinkling of fresh parsley and a generous dusting of Parmesan cheese, resulting in a flavor-packed meal that's quick and easy to prepare.

Ingredients

  • 200 grams of spaghetti
  • 2 large cloves of garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon of red chili flakes (or to taste)
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Parsley, chopped, for garnish
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for garnish

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a pot of salted boiling water until al dente, according to package instructions. Drain, reserving 1 cup of the pasta water.
  2. Meanwhile, in a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and chili flakes, cooking until the garlic is golden, about 2 minutes.
  3. Add the drained spaghetti, salt, and pepper to the skillet, tossing to combine. If needed, add the reserved pasta water a little at a time to loosen up the pasta.
  4. Remove from heat, garnish with parsley and grated Parmesan. Serve immediately. Enjoy your meal!
